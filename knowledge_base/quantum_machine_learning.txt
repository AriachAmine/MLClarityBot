## Definition

Quantum Machine Learning (QML) is a rapidly developing field that combines the principles of quantum computing with machine learning algorithms to potentially solve problems currently intractable for classical computers.  It leverages the unique properties of quantum mechanics to enhance the speed and efficiency of machine learning models.


## Explanation

Classical machine learning relies on bits representing 0 or 1.  Quantum machine learning uses qubits, which can represent 0, 1, or a superposition of both simultaneously due to quantum phenomena like superposition and entanglement. This allows quantum computers to explore a much larger solution space concurrently.  Certain machine learning tasks, like optimization problems or pattern recognition in extremely high-dimensional data, could see significant speedups using quantum algorithms.  However, it's important to note that QML is still in its early stages; building and programming quantum computers is incredibly challenging, and the advantages of QML aren't universally applicable to all machine learning problems.  Current research focuses on developing quantum algorithms tailored to specific machine learning tasks and building the necessary hardware.
